Skills that make a good leader in tech

There is an endless barrage of blogs and linkedin posts suggesting that the sign of someone being a good leader is that they are good listeners, exhibit empathy and lead from the front, giving all credit to the team and taking blame for any failures.

These traits suggest the leader has character. They are essential.

What about specific skills though? Good leaders that I encountered had specific skills that helped shape their success as a leader. Here are some:

  1. Ask good questions - technical, managerial, strategic questions.
  2. Follow-up effortlessly and continuously. This one goes against the grain of leadership gyan that professes good leaders are hands-off and just need to get out of the way. No, they don't.
  3. Not feel intimidated when their opinion or decision is challenged.
  4. Create a buzz about their team.
  5. Create real value for business. Align demand and supply perfectly.
  6. Don't wait to be told. Pre-empt business needs, problems, resource needs..
  7. Believe in some values. Instill them in their team. Build a culture and an army of talented, invested people, not just a pool of obedient servants.
  8. Have the courage to take difficult decisions and speak up.
  9. Represent their team on important platforms. Connect with other leaders.
  10. Remember personal and professional dates and times - birthdays, work anniversaries, recurring office events.
  11. Find time to connect with everyone in the team.
  12. Be really good at at least one technology used by the team.
