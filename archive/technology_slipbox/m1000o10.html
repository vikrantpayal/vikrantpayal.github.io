Machine Learning Concepts - Overfitting

What is Overfitting?

Overfitting happens when the model learns the data a little too well. Even noise or fluctuations in the data are part of the model's learnt dataset. When such a model is applied to a new, unseen data, it performs poorly. Kind of like if you memorize stuff for an exam without understanding anything. If the exam has a question that is not verbatim what you memorized, you are unable to answer.

To avoid overfitting, don't use too many parameters. Don't use a model that is too flexible relative to the quality of data.

A symptom of overfitting is high training accuracy but low test accuracy.
