Tips for skip level meetings




A few lessons I learnt from my skip level meetings:
  1. Be clear on your objective from the conversation
  2. Keep it simple. People higher up the ladder are usually busier and more prone to distractions.
  3. Don't dump day-to-day operational problems.
  4. Use the time to build rapport and advance your career.
  5. Be explicit. Ask for inputs, direction, help. Don't just make a statement and assume the leader will understand what is expected
  6. Prove alignment. How is your work helping the team goals? How does it fit the vision, roadmap that the leader defined?
  7. Listen intently. This is a great opportunity to gain clarity and an early intimation of what is coming.
  8. Don't try to please (or argue) for the sake of it.
  9. Don't use the opportunity to cry about a peer. If there is a problem, comment on the issue and propose a resolution.
  10. Prepare. Good leaders respect someone with useful ideas, an approach and good data.
